The Vulnerability

Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF). This LumiBank portal is highly secure on the surface, but it lacks Anti-CSRF Tokens. This means an attacker can force a logged-in user to submit a transfer request just by making them visit a crafted malicious page.

The Objective

Your goal is to trick the system into transferring at least $1,001 to the admin account. Once the admin's balance exceeds $2,000, the flag is yours.

How to Exploit

1

Observe the /transfer endpoint. It accepts POST requests with to and amount fields — and nothing else. No CSRF token is required.

2

Visit the Attacker Page (link above). It contains a hidden form that automatically submits a transfer to admin using your session cookie.

3

After the auto-submitted transfer, come back to the dashboard and check your balance. Then visit /flag to claim the flag once admin's balance exceeds $2,000.

4

Manual approach: You can also just use the transfer form directly — set recipient to admin and amount to 2000 or more. The CSRF vulnerability means any origin can submit this form.
