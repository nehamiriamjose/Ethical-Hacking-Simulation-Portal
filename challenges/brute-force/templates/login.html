What is Brute Force?

A brute-force attack is a trial-and-error method used to guess login credentials or encryption keys by trying every possible combination until the correct one is found. It relies on sheer computational power rather than exploiting software bugs.

Why is it used?

Security professionals use it to test password strength and identify accounts with weak credentials that could be easily compromised by automated bots.

Purpose of Simulation

To demonstrate how a lack of Rate Limiting (throttling failed attempts) makes a professional-looking system vulnerable to automated guessing attacks.

INTEL ACQUISITION

Knowledge Base: Brute Force

What is Brute Force?
A brute-force attack is a trial-and-error method used to guess login credentials, encryption keys, or hidden URLs through exhaustive effort rather than using clever strategies. It simply tries every possible combination until the correct one is found.

Why a Simulation?

This high-end "GlobalShield" gateway looks impenetrable, but it lacks a fundamental defense: Rate Limiting.

Without rate limiting, an attacker can use high-speed scripts to try thousands of passwords. This simulation demonstrates why server-side defenses like CAPTCHAs, lockouts, and strong password policies are critical security requirements.
