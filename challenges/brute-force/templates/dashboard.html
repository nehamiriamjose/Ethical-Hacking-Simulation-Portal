Knowledge Base: Brute Force

What is Brute Force?
A brute-force attack is a trial-and-error method used to guess login credentials, encryption keys, or hidden URLs. Instead of looking for intellectual flaws, it uses exhaustive effort to try every possible combination until the correct one is found.

Why a Simulation?

In this simulation, the "GlobalShield" gateway has a professional facade but lacks Rate Limiting and Account Lockout.

This allows an attacker to try common passwords (like "p@ssword123") indefinitely without the system blocking them. This simulation teaches why strong password policies and server-side throttles are mandatory.
